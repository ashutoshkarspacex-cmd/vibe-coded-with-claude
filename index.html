Our Story

Born from a
teenager's dream.

This page is the dream of a teenager from a small town — an idea born from a deep love for words and stories. It's an attempt to bring together aspiring writers from around the world, creating a platform that celebrates free will, creativity, and imagination.

At the heart of this space is independent thinking. That's what we strive to nurture, one story at a time.

We live in the age of AI — a time when the internet is overflowing with recycled ideas and altered versions of once-original works. While technology has its place, it has also made it harder to find the raw, unfiltered voice of a human mind at work.

Here, we take a different path. Every word you read on this platform is born from genuine human thought — crafted with intention, shaped by real emotions, and unpolished in all the ways that make writing truly alive.

We don't just want content. We want ideas. We want the spark that comes from a living, breathing person putting pen to paper and letting their mind roam free.

A Divine Request — COVID Edition

@HINDUTV · Satire · Mr. Fiction Zone

Dear MAN, you have one notification from Lord Vishnu. Please read carefully — this is not spam. The divine inbox rarely opens for mortals, and yet here we are.

"It has come to our divine attention that the chaos currently unfolding on Earth is... unprecedented. Even by our standards."

Lord Vishnu, seated atop his celestial throne, stared at the cosmic dashboard with an expression nobody had seen in millennia — bewilderment. The numbers were unusual. Temples empty, mosques silent, churches locked. For the first time in recorded divine history, humanity had collectively stopped arguing about which god to pray to and had united under one common enemy: a microscopic speck of protein.

"They are wearing masks," announced Garuda, peering through the celestial telescope. "Some on their faces. Some on their chins. A few on their foreheads. The creativity is admirable, though misguided."

The divine request was simple: stay home, stay safe, wash your hands. But the interpretation, as always with humans, was… creative. Some stockpiled toilet paper with the urgency of preparing for Armageddon. Others convinced themselves that a warm glass of turmeric milk was an antiviral shield. Social media pundits declared that 5G towers were to blame, apparently forgetting that conspiracy theories require at least a passing acquaintance with physics.

"We sent a pause button," Vishnu reportedly told the assembled devas. "They are using it to bake sourdough bread and argue on the internet."

And so it was written — in the divine ledger, in the chapter titled 'Humanity: An Ongoing Experiment' — that when the universe offered silence, man filled it with noise. When given rest, he invented stress. And when handed a pandemic, he somehow turned it into a political debate.

The gods, watching from above, did what gods do in times of profound confusion. They sighed. They marvelled. And somewhere, just between the galaxies, they quietly updated their notes: "Still unpredictable. Strangely resilient. Do not delete."

Satire · A Divine Request Series · Vol. 2

A Divine Request — The Chaos Around

@HINDUTV · 2025 · Mr. Fiction Zone

It's 2025. Lord Vishnu is back. And this time, he's brought a PowerPoint presentation.

The Celestial Control Room, which had undergone a significant renovation since the pandemic years (they added a second screen — things had escalated), was buzzing. Celestial analysts pointed at graphs. The lines all trended in the same direction: chaotic, upward, and frankly alarming.

"They survived the plague," said Narad Muni, scrolling through the divine news feed. "And then they immediately resumed everything that caused it."

Slide one of Vishnu's presentation was titled: "You Had One Job."

It was a busy year for humanity. Political theatre reached new heights — or rather depths — of spectacle. Wars that had been quietly simmering for decades suddenly demanded global attention simultaneously, as if they'd coordinated calendar entries. Economic systems creaked under the weight of choices made decades ago. Social media algorithms cheerfully fed everyone information specifically designed to confirm whatever they already believed, making the art of changing one's mind practically extinct.

The chaos was not new. The chaos had always been there. What was new was the volume — the sheer relentless decibel level of modern human experience, where every opinion required a platform, every grievance a hashtag, and every moment of potential quiet was immediately colonized by a notification.

"Silence," Vishnu told the assembled gods, "has become the rarest luxury on Earth. More precious than gold. More sought-after than wisdom."

Slide two was titled: "Nevertheless, They Persist."

Because between all the chaos — tucked between the headlines and the hot takes and the endless scrolling — something stubborn kept happening. People kept falling in love. Artists kept making things. Children kept asking impossible questions adults couldn't answer. Someone, somewhere, was always writing something down.

The gods watched. The chaos continued. And the divine verdict, unchanged: "Do not delete. They're still figuring it out."

A Divine Request — Indian Independence Day Special

@HINDUTV · Independence Day Edition · Mr. Fiction Zone

Lord Vishnu has a special wish for his exclusive members — Indians. What chaos will unfold now?

On the 15th of August, as the tricolour rose with practiced grace and schoolchildren rehearsed patriotic songs they'd been singing since they were four, Lord Vishnu convened an emergency meeting of the South Asian Divine Council.

"Seventy-eight years," he said, holding up the divine ledger. "Seventy-eight years of Independence, and they are still arguing about who is more patriotic."

The argument, as Narad Muni dutifully reported, had moved online. Twitter — renamed but not reformed — was ablaze. Who was standing for the national anthem? Who was sitting? Who was eating the wrong food? Who had posted their patriotism at the correct time of day? The nation's identity, it seemed, was less a shared inheritance and more a daily competitive examination.

Meanwhile, in the background of the performative patriotism, things of remarkable quiet beauty were also happening. A farmer in Maharashtra was up before dawn, tending to crops that would feed a city that would never know his name. A teacher in a small village was explaining democracy to children who took it more seriously than most adults. A street-food vendor in Old Delhi had, through some alchemy of spice and time, created a dish so perfect it briefly united two strangers who had nothing else in common.

"India," Vishnu told the council, "is not a country. It is an argument that refuses to end — and somehow, that is also its greatest strength."

The divine wish, delivered through the usual channels (a soft wind, a meaningful coincidence, a dream slightly too vivid to ignore), was simple: Remember what Independence actually means. It's not just freedom from something. It's freedom to become something.

The flag kept rising. The argument kept going. And somewhere beneath the noise, always, the quiet hum of a civilization that has survived everything — including itself.

Happy Independence Day.

#Operation
Humanity

In a distant future, after Earth succumbs to humanity's cruelty and environmental negligence, people discover a new beginning on Rablox — an uncharted Earth-like planet. But can humanity sustain this fresh opportunity, or will the cycle of destruction inevitably repeat itself?

From surfing in the wastelands to venturing into the complex political framework of a planet which re-breathed life into humanity. A planet that revamped the hope of a race whose entire history was ruined by their own actions, yet remains largely unexplored, housing myriads of mysteries hidden in its sands — lingering to be unearthed.

Characters

CHARACTER 01

Zetron

The Commander

The iron will of a new civilization. Zetron commands with the authority of someone who has seen what happens when authority collapses — and is determined not to let it happen again on Rablox. Strategic, scarred, and carrying the weight of a species that nearly destroyed itself.

CHARACTER 02

Rakun

The God

Mysterious, ancient, and bound to Rablox in ways that defy human understanding. Rakun exists at the intersection of myth and reality — a force the colonists cannot explain, cannot control, and cannot afford to ignore. Is Rakun a saviour, a test, or something else entirely?
